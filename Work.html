PARALLAX SCROLLING EFFECT

Parallax scrolling is an animation technique in which background images move slower than the images in the foreground, creating an illusion of depth in an otherwise 2D scene. In order to get a better understanding of the concept, it helps to look into where the word comes from.
A parallax ('parallax' is derived from the Greek word παράλλαξις (parallaxis), meaning 'alternation') refers to a displacement of an object due to a change in the observer’s point of view.
